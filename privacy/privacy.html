The short version: Kippit stores all your financial data locally on your device. We do not collect it, we cannot see it, and we never sell it. No account is required to use the app.

Overview

Kippit (“the app”, “we”, “our”) is a personal expense tracking application for iPhone developed by Erica Cheong and distributed at kipp-it.app.

This Privacy Policy explains what information the app handles, how it is stored, and your rights regarding that information. By using Kippit, you agree to this policy.

Data we collect

Kippit stores the following data, but only locally on your device and optionally in your private iCloud account. This data is never transmitted to us or any third party:

  • Expense records — amount, category, payment method, date, optional note
  • Repayment records — amount, account, type, date, optional note
  • Transfer records — amount, from/to payment methods, date, optional note
  • Scheduled entry definitions — name, amount, interval, due dates
  • App settings — categories, payment methods, favorites, budget, currency preference

All data is stored using Apple’s SwiftData framework on your device. You have complete ownership and control. You can export, delete, or clear all data at any time from within the app.

Data we never collect

Kippit does not collect, transmit, store on any server, or have access to:

  • Your name, email address, or any personal identifiers
  • Your bank account numbers or financial institution credentials
  • Your device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV, or similar)
  • Your location
  • Usage patterns, screen recordings, or analytics
  • Crash reports or diagnostic data sent to us
  • Any data entered into the app

There is no registration, no login, and no server that receives data from Kippit.

iCloud sync

Kippit offers optional iCloud sync. When enabled:

  • Your data is stored in your personal iCloud account using Apple’s CloudKit framework
  • It is accessible only to you and devices signed into the same Apple ID
  • The developer has no access to your iCloud data
  • Apple’s privacy policy governs how iCloud stores and handles your data

iCloud sync is optional and can be disabled at any time in Settings. Turning it off keeps your data on this device only. Data already stored in iCloud is not deleted when you disable sync — manage it through your iPhone’s iCloud settings.

Third-party services

Kippit does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or data collection services. The app makes no network requests except for optional iCloud sync via Apple’s CloudKit.

Kippit does not display advertisements. No advertising SDKs are included in the app.

The app is distributed through Apple’s App Store. Apple may collect certain data as part of the App Store distribution process, governed by Apple’s own privacy policy.

Children’s privacy

Kippit is rated 4+ on the App Store and is suitable for all ages. Because we collect no personal data from any user, there are no special provisions required for users under 13. No data from children or any other users is collected or transmitted.

Changes to this policy

If this policy is updated, the new version will be posted at kipp-it.app/privacy with a revised “Last updated” date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Because Kippit collects no personal data, any policy changes are expected to be minor and administrative in nature.
